Nigeria Jollof Rice

This vibrant and flavor Nigeria jollof rice is made with rice, tomatoes, peppers, and spices, giving it a rich taste and beautiful orange-red color. It is often served at parties and celebrations, making it a beloved dish in Nigerian cuisine. The recipe is easy to prepare and can be a great addition to any meal.
Jollof rice is a staple in many West African countries, but the Nigerian version is particularly famous for its unique blend of spices and flavors. It is a popular dish that is often enjoyed with fried plantains, chicken, or salad. The combination of spices and the smoky flavor of the rice makes it a unique and memorable dish.

Recipe Information

Preparation Time: 20 minutes

Cooking Time: 30 minutes

Servings: 4-6 people

Ingredients

  • 3 cups uncooked white rice
  • 1/4 vegetable oil
  • 6 large tomatoes (blended)
  • 3 red bell peppers (blended)
  • 2 medium onions (1 blended, 1 sliced)
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (blended)
  • 3 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1/4 cup diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups chicken stock
  • 1 tablespoons thyme
  • 1 tablespoons curry powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 seasoning cubes
  • 1 bay leaf

Instructions

  1. Blend tomatoes, red bell peppers, scotch bonnets, and one onion into a smooth mixture.
  2. Heat oil in a large pot, add sliced onions, and fry for 2 minutes.
  3. Add tomato paste and fry for 3–5 minutes to reduce acidity.
  4. Pour in the blended mixture, season with thyme, curry powder, seasoning cubes, and bay leaf, then cook for 10–15 minutes.
  5. Stir in chicken stock and bring to a boil.
  6. Add washed parboiled rice, stir well, cover, and cook on low heat for 20–25 minutes.
  7. Check occasionally, adding a little water or stock if needed until rice is tender.
  8. Fluff the rice and serve hot with fried plantains, chicken, or salad.

Additional Information

For a smokier taste, let the rice steam on low heat for a few extra minutes after cooking or in the oven or using local fired wood to cook it. — this is the secret to Party Jollof Rice.
